ARBROATH, ANGUS

Founded in 1178. The home of Arbroath Smokies, the Declaration of Independence and Arbroath F.C.

History of Arbroath

Paragraph

Timeline of Historic Events

Year Event
10,000-2,200 BC Radiocarbon evidence suggests that the area of Arbroath has been inhabitated since the New Stone Age.
400-500s The area of Arbroath is inhabitated by Picts, an ancient group of Scottish people.
1128 Arbroath Abbey is founded by King William the Lionheart.
1214 King William the Lionheart is buried in the grounds of the Arbroath Abbey.
1233 The construction of Arbroath Abbey reached completion.
1320 The Declaration of Arbroath is sent to Pope John XXII, and Scotland is declared an independent nation.
1446 The Battle of Arbroath ensues between Clan Lindsay and their rivals, resulting in the death of at least 600 people.
1560 The abbey is dissolved during Reformation and falls into dissrepair, with stones of the building stolen for house construction by local residents.
1599 James VI announces Arbroath as a royal burgh.
1745 Jacobite Rising leads to Arbroath becoming a Jacobite town.
1815 The Abbey is taken into state care and restored.
1839 The fishing industry in Arbroath grows, whereby the council brought in fishermen from nearby Auchmithie and the Shetland Isles.
1875 Jacobite Rising leads to Arbroath becoming a Jacobite town.
1916 Arbroath Infirmary, originally opened in 1845, is reformed as a new healthcare premises in the town.

Declaration of Arbroath

Written in 1320, this document sent to the pope secured Scottish Independence from the English.
